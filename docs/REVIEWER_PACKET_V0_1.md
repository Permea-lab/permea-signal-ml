## Reviewer-type guidance

- computational reviewer: prefer the extended packet; most useful feedback concerns benchmark framing, artifact structure, reproducibility, and claim boundary
- biologically literate skeptical reviewer: minimum packet is usually sufficient; most useful feedback concerns overclaim risk, biological plausibility framing, and limitations
- delivery/peptide domain reviewer: minimum packet plus selected support docs; most useful feedback concerns scope discipline and whether the candidate-prioritization framing is reasonable
- general technical clarity reviewer: minimum packet; most useful feedback concerns readability, coherence, and whether the manuscript can be followed without insider context

## What the packet is trying to test

- claim-boundary clarity
- manuscript coherence
- evidence legibility
- usefulness of benchmark framing
- adequacy of limitations
- whether the imported-versus-regenerated distinction is understandable

## What the packet is not asking the reviewer to judge

The packet is not asking reviewers to assess the work as a wet-lab validation paper, a therapeutic efficacy paper, a solved-delivery claim, or a final submission-ready manuscript with finalized metadata.

## Immediate use guidance

Use the minimum packet for first-pass trusted review when you want fast signal on clarity and claim boundaries. Use the extended packet when the reviewer is likely to inspect the package structure, benchmark logic, or provenance-facing support material in more detail.

For a compact map of the review-operation documents, see `REVIEW_OPERATIONS_INDEX_V0_1.md`.

For first-wave execution, use `FIRST_REVIEW_WAVE_PLAN_V0_1.md` and `FIRST_REVIEW_WAVE_CHECKLIST_V0_1.md`. For feedback triage after comments arrive, use `REVISION_PRIORITY_FRAMEWORK_V0_1.md`.
